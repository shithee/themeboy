Cricket

Cricket is a popular sport played in many countries around the world. It is a bat-and-ball game that is similar to baseball, but with some differences in the rules and gameplay.

History of Cricket

Cricket has a long and interesting history that dates back to the 16th century. It originated in England and has since become a popular sport in many other countries.

Rules of Cricket

  • Cricket is played with two teams of 11 players each. The game is divided into innings, with each team getting a chance to bat and bowl.
  • The batting team scores runs by hitting the ball and running between two sets of wickets, while the bowling team tries to get the batting team out by catching the ball or hitting the wickets with the ball.
  • The team that scores the most runs at the end of the game wins. If the game ends in a tie, a tie-breaker is played.

Equipment for Cricket

  1. Bat: The cricket bat is usually made of wood and has a flat front surface with a slightly curved back. It is used by the batsman to hit the ball.
  2. Ball: The cricket ball is made of cork covered with leather. It is hard and can be bowled at high speeds by the bowler.
  3. Stumps: The stumps are three vertical posts that are placed in the ground. The bails are placed on top of the stumps.
  4. Pads: The pads are worn by the batsman to protect their legs from the ball.
  5. Gloves: The gloves are worn by the batsman to protect their hands from the ball.
  6. Helmets: The helmets are worn by the batsman and the wicket-keeper to protect their head and face from the ball.
